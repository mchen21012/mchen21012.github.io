About Me

I am a Master of Engineering (M.Eng) student in Computer Science at Cornell University's College of Engineering. My concentration is in Natural Language Processing, but I am also greatly interested in video game development and artificial intelligence. For my undergraduate studies, I attended Cornell University's College of Arts and Sciences and majored in English and Information Science with minors in Computer Science and Education. My primary interest in English is 20th Century American and British Literature, but I also have a strong interest in American theatre. In Information Science, my concentration was in Networks, Crowds, and Markets.

Outside of class, I am the vice president of Cornell Piano Society and the secretary and webmaster of Von Cramm Cooperative Hall. As someone who wishes to continue her passion for piano, I teach and mentor students interested in piano. I also accompanied for Cornell's theatre production of Mr. Burns. In my free time, I like to brainstorm new story ideas and write about them as well as read and analyze prose literature and psychology texts.

Goals in the Future

Video Game Writing/Scripting and Web Development

One of my future goals is to become a professional video game writer. Ever since I was a child, I have been enamored by video games. In particular, I am fascinated by Japanese role-playing games, which heavily depend on a storyline. As a creative writer, I would love to utilize my writing skills to create new and unique games for people to play. Moreover, I would also like to integrate educational aspects into video games. Children should not be prevented from having fun; thus, I hope that I can create video games that are both fun and intellectually stimulating. Furthermore, I want to make games that combat stereotypes and harmful norms as video games can greatly shape the ideologies of the players. Playing video games has brought so much joy into my life and has made me the person I am today. As a result, I hope to spread this joy to the future generation of gamers.

In addition, I hope that I can continue to fully employ my web development skills. Like many others, I find it frustrating to navigate and engage with websites with poor user interfaces and unclear affordances. Therefore, it brings me great joy to create accessible websites and ensure great user experience. Creating websites can be time-consuming and expensive; consequently, I hope to offer my services as a web developer to clients who need a website to promote their cause or business.


Education and Literature

One of my goals in the future is to become an educator in literature. My middle school and high school English teachers played an essential role in inciting my interest in reading and analyzing literature. Consequently, I would like to do the same by spreading my passion for literature to the future generation of students. Furthermore, more and more people (including myself) are pursuing an education and career in STEM. However, some people who are in STEM feel obligated to stay on this track because of the stigma that the humanities are insignificant and useless. However, literature, history, art and philosophy have played an integral part in shaping our society. Therefore, I want to be an individual who educates others on the importance and beauty of literature and the arts. I hope that more people will discover the joys of reading and writing and the impact literature has on the world.

Furthermore, I believe that we can do much more in ensuring that all students, regardless of class, gender, sexual orientation, race or religion, can receive a quality education. Using culturally relevant teaching methods and theories of critical pedagogy, I believe that I can make a significant impact on the lives of youths who genuinely desire to learn.
